STEP ONE: Setting goals

Gain a comprehensive understanding of fundamental tools in front-end development.

  • Integrate subtle animations to elevate the user experience.
  • Implement a dedicated blog section to offer a glimpse behind the scenes.
  • Deepen knowledge and proficiency in creating mobile-friendly layouts.

STEP TWO: Figma design

Learning more about Figma

In my bootcamp I found that at least having a fundmental understanding of wire framing, UI and UX can help alot in the early planning stages of any project. Being able to communicate your ideas effectively gives a team or in my case an individual a clear direction before the coding begins.

In this case I decided to do Figma UI UX Design Essentials by Daniel Scott on Skillshare. I found this course to be very helpful and I was able to apply the skills I learned to my portfolio website. I was able to create a wireframe and a mockup of my website before I started coding.

STEP 3: Challenges and obstacles

Here are some common ones I have faced so far in this project:

  • Sorting web and mobile layouts early is crucial to prevent complications. Prioritise layout planning at the beginning of the project to streamline development.
  • More of an insight here but you quickly learn to appreciate React once you find yourself not using it in your projects.
  • I originally had a contact form in my original design but felt that it was a bit over kill for this project. Do not be afraid to edit your original ideas but make sure to keep a record of your changes.
  • I found that I was spending too much time on one task and not enough time on another. I found that the best way to overcome this is to set a time limit for each task and to stick to it.
